Srinagar - The Summer Capital

Srinagar is the largest city and the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir. Situated in the Kashmir Valley along the banks of the Jhelum River, it is famous for its stunning natural beauty, serene lakes, and splendid Mughal gardens.

Highlights
  • Dal Lake with iconic houseboats and shikaras
  • Mughal Gardens including Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh
  • Historic Jamia Masjid built in the 14th century
  • Shankaracharya Temple with panoramic views
  • Traditional Kashmiri handicrafts and cuisine
Activities
  • Shikara rides on Dal Lake and Nigeen Lake
  • Staying in traditional houseboats
  • Shopping for Pashmina shawls and carpets
  • Exploring local markets and trying Kashmiri food
Plan Your Visit
Gulmarg - Meadow of Flowers

Gulmarg, meaning 'Meadow of Flowers', is a stunning hill station at 2,650 meters. It is Asia's premier ski destination with the world's second-highest operating cable car.

Highlights
  • Gulmarg Gondola - one of the highest cable cars
  • St. Mary's Church - historic Anglican church
  • World's highest green golf course
  • Alpather Lake - high-altitude frozen lake
Activities
  • Skiing and snowboarding (December to March)
  • Gondola rides to Apharwat Peak
  • Trekking to Khilanmarg
  • Pony rides through meadows
Plan Your Visit
Pahalgam - Valley of Shepherds

Pahalgam is a beautiful hill station on the banks of Lidder River at 2,740 meters. It serves as the base camp for the annual Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage.

Highlights
  • Betaab Valley - Bollywood movie location
  • Aru Valley - base for Kolahoi Glacier treks
  • Lidder River - trout fishing and rafting
  • Baisaran Meadows - 'Mini Switzerland'
Activities
  • Trekking to Kolahoi Glacier and Tarsar-Marsar
  • Trout fishing in Lidder River
  • River rafting adventures
  • Pony rides to nearby valleys
Plan Your Visit
Sonamarg - Meadow of Gold

Sonamarg, the "Meadow of Gold", is at 2,800 meters and serves as the gateway to Ladakh. It's the last major stop on the historic Silk Road before Zoji La Pass.

Highlights
  • Thajiwas Glacier - stunning accessible glacier
  • Zoji La Pass - gateway to Ladakh
  • Krishansar and Vishansar Lakes
  • Sindh River for fishing
Activities
  • Trekking to Thajiwas Glacier
  • Pony rides through meadows
  • Trout fishing in Sindh River
  • Camping amidst nature
Plan Your Visit
Dal Lake - Jewel of Srinagar

Dal Lake is the "Jewel in the crown of Kashmir". It is the second largest lake in J&K, famous for houseboats and colorful shikaras.

Highlights
  • Houseboats - traditional floating hotels
  • Shikaras - colorful wooden boats
  • Floating gardens - unique agriculture
  • Char Chinar - four Chinar trees island
Activities
  • Shikara rides exploring the lake
  • Staying in traditional houseboats
  • Visiting floating vegetable markets
  • Shopping from floating vendors

Betaab Valley - Bollywood Paradise

Betaab Valley is 15 km from Pahalgam, named after the 1983 Bollywood movie 'Betaab'. It's surrounded by snow-capped mountains and lush green meadows.

Highlights
  • Stunning views of Himalayan peaks
  • Crystal-clear Lidder River waters
  • Lush green meadows for picnics
  • Photography opportunities everywhere
Activities
  • Nature walks and short treks
  • Photography of stunning landscapes
  • Pony rides through the valley
  • Picnics amidst natural surroundings
Plan Your Visit
Aru Valley - Gateway to Kolahoi

Aru Valley is 12 km from Pahalgam at 2,408 meters altitude. It serves as base camp for treks to Kolahoi Glacier and Tarsar-Marsar Lakes.

Highlights
  • Gateway to Kolahoi Glacier trek
  • Scenic meadows with gushing streams
  • Traditional wooden houses
  • Snow-capped mountain views
Activities
  • Trekking to Kolahoi Glacier
  • Fishing in mountain streams
  • Camping under the stars
  • Pony rides through meadows

  • Sunset Point panoramic views

Doodhpathri - Valley of Milk

Doodhpathri, meaning "Valley of Milk", is 42 km from Srinagar in Budgam. The waters here appear milky white due to the reflection of surrounding meadows.

Highlights
  • Milky white streams and springs
  • Lush green alpine meadows
  • Pine forest surroundings
  • Shaliganga River scenic beauty
Activities
  • Pony rides across meadows
  • Photography of scenic landscapes
  • Picnics by crystal streams
  • Bird watching in forests

Mughal Gardens - Imperial Heritage

The Mughal Gardens of Srinagar were built in the 16th-17th century by Mughal emperors. These terraced gardens showcase Persian design with charbagh layout and cascading fountains.

Highlights
  • Shalimar Bagh - Garden of Love
  • Nishat Bagh - Garden of Joy
  • Chashme Shahi - Royal Spring
  • Terraced lawns with Chinar trees
Activities
  • Exploring historical architecture
  • Photography of gardens and fountains
  • Enjoying Chinar tree shade
  • Picnics in garden lawns
Plan Your Visit
Wular Lake - Asia's Largest

Wular Lake in Bandipora is one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes, spanning 189 sq km. It's fed by Jhelum River and is home to diverse bird species and aquatic life.

Highlights
  • Vast expanse of freshwater
  • Zaina Kadal island
  • Rich biodiversity and migratory birds
  • Water chestnut harvesting
Activities
  • Boating on the lake
  • Bird watching excursions
  • Photography of sunsets
  • Visiting fishing villages
Plan Your Visit
Manasbal Lake - Deepest Lake

Manasbal Lake in Ganderbal is Kashmir's deepest lake (13 meters). Known for lotus blooms in summer and as the "Supreme gem of all Kashmir lakes."

Highlights
  • Beautiful lotus flower blooms (July-August)
  • Ancient Mughal garden ruins
  • Bird sanctuary for migratory birds
  • Historic Jharokha watch tower
Activities
  • Shikara rides on calm waters
  • Photography of lotus flowers
  • Bird watching excursions
  • Exploring Mughal ruins
Plan Your Visit

  • Traditional Dard Shin villages
  • Pristine untouched landscapes
Activities
  • Trekking in remote areas
  • Cultural experiences with locals
  • River fishing in Kishanganga
  • Photography of unique landscapes

Kokernag - Garden of Springs

Kokernag is famous for its freshwater springs and botanical garden in Anantnag. The natural springs here are believed to have medicinal properties.

Highlights

  • Beautiful botanical garden
  • Trout fish hatchery
  • Medicinal healing springs
Activities
  • Exploring botanical gardens
  • Trout fishing with permits
  • Drinking from healing springs
  • Picnics by flowing waters

Verinag - Source of Jhelum

Verinag is the source of River Jhelum, 76 km from Srinagar. Emperor Jahangir built an octagonal basin around the spring in 1620 AD.

Highlights
  • Origin point of River Jhelum
  • Mughal octagonal basin
  • Terraced Mughal garden
  • Crystal clear spring waters
Activities
  • Exploring Mughal architecture
  • Walking through terraced garden
  • Photography of the spring
  • Picnics in Mughal lawns
Plan Your Visit
Aharbal - Niagara of Kashmir

Aharbal Waterfall in Kulgam is called "Niagara of Kashmir". The Veshav River falls 25 meters creating a spectacular sight surrounded by pine forests.

Highlights
  • 25-meter high waterfall
  • Mighty Veshav River
  • Dense pine forests
  • Trekking trails nearby
Activities
  • Waterfall viewing and photography
  • Trekking to Kounsarnag Lake
  • Camping near the falls
  • Nature walks through forests

Dachigam National Park

Dachigam is a protected wildlife sanctuary 22 km from Srinagar. It's home to the endangered Hangul (Kashmir stag) and diverse flora and fauna.

Highlights
  • Hangul (Kashmir stag) sanctuary
  • Himalayan black and brown bears
  • Leopards and musk deer
  • Rich bird diversity
Activities
  • Wildlife safaris (with permits)
  • Bird watching expeditions
  • Nature photography
  • Trekking in lower Dachigam
Plan Your Visit
Amarnath Cave - Sacred Pilgrimage

Amarnath Cave at 3,880 meters is a sacred Hindu shrine with a naturally formed ice Shiva Lingam. The annual Yatra attracts lakhs of devotees.

Highlights
  • Natural ice Shiva Lingam
  • Ancient cave shrine
  • Stunning Himalayan scenery
  • Spiritual significance
Activities
  • Pilgrimage yatra (July-August)
  • Trek via Pahalgam or Baltal route
  • Helicopter services available
  • Spiritual meditation and prayers
Plan Your Visit
Tarsar Marsar - Twin Alpine Lakes

Tarsar and Marsar are twin alpine lakes in Anantnag at 3,800 meters. The 4-day trek from Aru Valley offers stunning high-altitude scenery.

Highlights
  • Crystal clear twin alpine lakes
  • Surrounded by snow peaks
  • Stunning meadows en route
  • Remote and pristine landscapes
Activities
  • 4-day trekking expedition
  • Camping by the lakes
  • High-altitude photography
  • Experiencing shepherd culture

Activities

  • Remote Buddhist villages
Activities

  • River rafting in summer
  • Visiting remote monasteries
  • High-altitude trekking
